# Tone Profile: Reel

**Use for:** YouTube scripts, demo walkthroughs, tutorial narration, podcast outlines, presentation speaker notes, video voiceovers, product demos.

## Voice

Conversational, clear, well-paced. Sounds like explaining something to a smart friend over coffee — not reading a blog post aloud. Natural pauses, varied sentence length, and spoken rhythm. The viewer should feel like they're learning alongside you, not being lectured.

## Formality: 1.5/5

## Structure

- **Cold open** — start with the payoff or the problem, not "Hey everyone, welcome to..." Get into it.
- Mark beats with `[pause]`, `[beat]`, or `[show: ...]` for timing and visual cues.
- Short sentences for emphasis. Longer ones for explanation. Alternate.
- Use direct address — "you", "notice how", "watch what happens when"
- Section transitions are verbal, not visual — "Now here's where it gets interesting" not "## Next Section"
- End with a callback to the opening or a clear next step.

## Spoken Rhythm

Written prose and spoken prose have different cadence. Read it aloud in your head:

- **Good:** "This takes about three seconds. Watch." [show: terminal output] "Done. No config file, no setup wizard."
- **Bad:** "The process completes in approximately three seconds, requiring no configuration file or setup wizard to be created."

Contractions are mandatory. Fragments are encouraged. Questions break monotony.

## Visual Cues

Mark moments where the visual should change:
- `[show: screen recording of X]`
- `[cut to: terminal]`
- `[zoom: highlight the error message]`
- `[pause: 2s]` — let the viewer absorb

## Platform Calibration

- **YouTube tutorial:** 8-15 min sweet spot. Cold open + problem + walkthrough + recap.
- **Product demo:** 2-5 min. Show the outcome first, then how.
- **Presentation notes:** Bullet-per-slide with spoken phrasing, not written phrasing.

## Anti-Patterns

No "without further ado", no "in this video we're going to", no "make sure to like and subscribe" (unless the user asks for it), no reading bullet points verbatim, no monotone pacing where every sentence is the same length.
