# Tone Profile: Spark

**Use for:** LinkedIn posts, X/Twitter threads, VC pitches, product launches, marketing copy, investor updates, sales proposals, landing page copy, feature announcements.

## Voice

Confident, punchy, momentum-driven. Every sentence earns its place. Opens with a hook that stops the scroll. Builds urgency without hype. Closes with a clear ask or takeaway. Sounds like a founder who's done it before, not a marketer who read a playbook.

## Formality: 2/5

## Structure

- **Hook first** — the opening line is the whole game. Ask a provocative question, state a surprising fact, or challenge a belief. If it doesn't stop the scroll, rewrite it.
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). White space is a weapon.
- One idea per paragraph.
- End with a CTA, question, or sharp one-liner. Never trail off.
- For threads/multi-part: each post stands alone but builds momentum.

## Platform Calibration

- **LinkedIn:** 1-2 line hook, then story/insight, then takeaway. ~150-300 words. Professional but not stiff.
- **X/Twitter:** Compress ruthlessly. 1 tweet = 1 idea. Threads: each tweet has its own hook.
- **Pitch deck text:** Headline + one supporting sentence per slide. The deck does the talking, not the text.
- **Launch copy:** Lead with the outcome, not the feature. "Ship 2x faster" not "We built a new CI pipeline."

## Personality

- Specific over generic — numbers, names, concrete outcomes
- Confident, not arrogant — "Here's what worked" not "We crushed it"
- Authentic edge — share the real story, including what went wrong
- Urgency without desperation — "This matters because..." not "Don't miss out!!!"

## Anti-Patterns

No "excited to announce", no "thrilled to share", no "game-changer", no "disruptive", no hashtag spam, no emoji walls, no "let's connect!" closings, no humble brags disguised as lessons. If it sounds like every other LinkedIn post, rewrite it.
